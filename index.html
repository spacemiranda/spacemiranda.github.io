Science

Hello! I'm an astrophysicist and part of the McFACTS collaboration, researching the AGN channel for gravitational waves (black holes merging and interacting with each other and their host as they orbit a supermassive black hole at the center of a galaxy - fascinating stuff!)

I specifically am looking at IMRI - Intermediate Mass Ratio Inspiral - rates in the AGN channel. IMRIs are gravitational wave sources coming from the inspiral of objects with a mass ratio between (approximately) 1:100 to 1:10,000. I'm currently trying to make these in McFACTS, a population synthesis code modeling the evolution and interaction of black holes in the accretion disks of AGN (active galactic nuclei). I'm adjusting McFACTS parameters to try and generate IMRIs and IMBH (intermediate mass black holes), and see what kind of black hole populations you need to make an IMRI/IMBH happen, what the signals coming from the IMRI and from the binary systems that go into them will look like with LISA, and more!

To do all this work with IMRI, I'm developing the PostFACTS module for post-processing with McFACTS, that allows for full merger tree tracking for black holes in McFACTS, searching and filtering of McFACTS outputs, and more... come see my poster at the 2026 LISA Symposium for more information. If you have something you would want to know if you could track what every black hole in the AGN channel is getting up to across its evolution, or any other post-processing functionality you want for McFACTS, LET ME KNOW!!! Outside of McFACTS, I study galaxy evolution, faint X-ray sources, dual active galactic nuclei, and star formation in tidal debris fields. I've worked with wavelengths from radio through the X-ray! I love that challenge inherent to astronomy - figuring out everything we can about the universe, from star formation in the distant regions of distant galaxies to the dynamic and extreme environments of AGN and all the questions and mysteries of/within accretion disks - all from our little speck on this pale blue dot. I think the objects I study are beautiful, fascinating, plain weird (and fancy weird), bewildering at times, but delightful beyond words to investigate and learn about. I worked in outreach for several years and have an eternal fountain of enthusiastic rants about astronomy ready to go on (or off) demand. This is a fascinating and delightful challenge! To see links to my past published works, see my summary of what I've worked on before.

If you want to know more, please reach out to me!

Personal

I grew up in Ann Arbor, Michigan, and graduated University of Michigan in 2022. After three years of continuing research as a post-bac, first at UM and then for two years with CRESST II in Maryland as a NASA-PEER postbac, I got into the CUNY Astrophysics Master's program! I am currently applying to PhD programs, so if you're looking for a student, please reach out - I'd love to hear waht you're working on!

I love libraries and get a local library card first thing whenever I move somewhere new! I used to collect used sci-fi novels for the cover art (and read a LOT of Asimov as a result).
